# IoTDash — Sales & Go-to-Market Strategy

> **Purpose:** Who to sell to, how to find them, how to pitch, what they care about, how to win, and what most IoT companies get wrong.

---

## 1. Who to Sell To

### 1.1 Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Your ideal early customer is NOT a Fortune 500. It's a **mid-size company (50-500 employees) that already has equipment they care about but monitors it manually or not at all.**

| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Avoid (for now) |
|-----------|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Company size | 50-500 employees | 20-50 or 500-2000 | <20 (can't pay) or >2000 (12-month sales cycle) |
| Technical maturity | Has IT department but no IoT team | Has one "tech-savvy" ops manager | Either fully DIY or fully outsourced IT |
| Current monitoring | Manual checks, paper logs, Excel | Basic SCADA, no remote access | Already has a competitor's IoT platform |
| Pain | Unplanned downtime costs them money | Compliance requires monitoring records | "Nice to have" with no urgency |
| Budget authority | Ops manager or plant manager can sign | Needs VP approval but <$50K | Requires board approval |
| Decision speed | Can decide in 2-4 weeks | 1-3 months | 6-12 months procurement process |

### 1.2 Target Industries (Ranked by Fit)

| # | Industry | Why They Buy | Typical Use Case | Price Sensitivity | Sales Cycle |
|---|----------|-------------|------------------|:-:|:-:|
| 1 | **Cold chain / Food storage** | Regulatory compliance (HACCP, FDA) — they MUST monitor | Freezer/cooler temperature monitoring | Medium | Short (2-4 wk) |
| 2 | **Warehousing / Logistics** | Perishable goods, insurance requirements | Temperature, humidity in storage | Medium | Short |
| 3 | **Commercial HVAC** | Energy costs, tenant complaints | Building temperature, energy usage | Low | Medium (4-8 wk) |
| 4 | **Agriculture / Greenhouses** | Crop loss prevention | Soil moisture, temperature, humidity | High | Short |
| 5 | **Water/Wastewater utilities** | Regulatory, environmental compliance | pH, flow rate, tank levels | Low | Medium |
| 6 | **Small manufacturing** | Unplanned downtime = lost production | Machine vibration, temperature, current | Medium | Medium |
| 7 | **Facility management** | Energy optimization, comfort | HVAC, lighting, occupancy | Medium | Medium |
| 8 | **Pharmacy / Labs** | Strict regulatory (GDP, GLP) | Fridge/freezer temperature for medications | Low | Medium |
| 9 | **Aquaculture / Fish farming** | Stock loss prevention | Water temperature, oxygen, pH | Medium | Short |
| 10 | **Rental / Property management** | Prevent water damage, mold | Leak detection, humidity | Medium | Short |

**Start with #1-3.** Cold chain and warehousing have the strongest forcing function: compliance. They don't buy because it's cool — they buy because they'll fail an audit without it.

---

### 1.3 Buyer Personas

| Persona | Title | Cares About | Afraid Of | How They Buy |
|---------|-------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Operations Manager** | Ops Manager, Plant Manager, Facility Manager | Uptime, efficiency, fewer fire drills | Equipment failure during their shift, audit failures | Wants to see a demo, then a pilot on 2-3 devices |
| **Quality/Compliance Manager** | QA Manager, Food Safety Manager, Compliance Officer | Audit trail, reports, proof of compliance | Failed audit, regulatory fine, recall | Needs PDF reports, data retention, export capability |
| **Owner / GM** | Owner, General Manager, Managing Director | Cost savings, ROI, competitive advantage | Spending money with no return, vendor lock-in | Wants a 1-page ROI calculation, references from similar companies |
| **IT Manager** | IT Manager, Sys Admin | Integration, security, not another thing to maintain | Security breach, unreliable system that generates support tickets | Wants to know about data security, hosting, SLA, API access |

**The buyer is usually the Ops Manager. The blocker is usually IT. The signer is usually the Owner/GM.**

## 2. Where to Find Them

### 2.1 Direct Outreach Channels

| Channel | How | Expected Response Rate | Cost |
|---------|-----|:---:|:---:|
| **LinkedIn** | Search by title + industry. "Operations Manager" + "cold storage" in your region. Send personalized connection request. | 5-15% accept, 2-5% respond | Free / $80/mo (Sales Navigator) |
| **Industry directories** | IARW (cold storage), IFDA (food distribution), local chamber of commerce member lists | 1-3% if cold emailing | Free |
| **Google Maps** | Search "cold storage [city]", "warehouse [city]", "food processing [city]". Visit websites, find contact info. | 2-5% if cold calling | Free |
| **Trade associations** | Join local industry associations. Attend member events. | High (warm intros) | $200-500/yr membership |
| **Referrals** | Every client you land → ask "Who else do you know that has this problem?" | 20-40% | Free |
| **Supplier partnerships** | HVAC installers, refrigeration companies, electrical contractors. They visit these sites daily. | Varies | Revenue share or referral fee |

### 2.2 Inbound Channels

| Channel | What to Do | Time to Results |
|---------|-----------|:---:|
| **Google Ads** | Bid on "temperature monitoring system", "cold storage monitoring", "remote equipment monitoring" | 1-2 weeks |
| **SEO / Blog** | Write: "HACCP temperature monitoring requirements 2026", "How to pass a cold storage audit", "Remote freezer monitoring" | 3-6 months |
| **YouTube** | 3-minute demo video: "How to monitor your cold room from your phone" | 1-3 months |
| **Industry forums / Reddit** | Answer questions in r/HVAC, r/foodsafety, r/farming, r/PLC. Be helpful, not salesy. | 1-3 months |
| **LinkedIn content** | Post case studies, before/after stories, industry tips weekly | 2-4 months |

### 2.3 Events & In-Person

| Event Type | Examples | Why It Works |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Trade shows** | IARW Convention, National Restaurant Association Show, local food safety expos | Decision-makers attend. They're already thinking about solutions. |
| **Local business meetups** | BNI groups, chamber of commerce events | Referral networks. The HVAC guy knows the warehouse owner. |
| **Facility tours** | Offer a free "monitoring assessment" — walk their facility, identify risks | You see their pain firsthand. They feel understood. |
| **Webinars** | "5 Signs Your Cold Storage Monitoring Is Outdated" | Capture leads who self-identify as having the problem |

**Best ROI for a solo founder: LinkedIn outreach + referral partnerships with HVAC/refrigeration installers.**

## 3. How to Pitch

### 3.1 The 30-Second Elevator Pitch

**Template:**
> "We help [industry] companies monitor their [equipment] remotely so they never [painful consequence] again. Our clients typically [quantified benefit] within [timeframe]."

**Examples:**

> **Cold storage:** "We help food storage companies monitor freezer temperatures 24/7 from their phone, so they never fail another HACCP audit or lose a shipment to a temperature excursion. Our clients typically save 10-20 hours per week of manual logging within the first month."

> **Manufacturing:** "We help manufacturers monitor machine health remotely, so they catch problems before equipment fails. Our clients typically reduce unplanned downtime by 30-40% in the first quarter."

> **HVAC:** "We help facility managers track building temperatures and energy usage in real-time, so they can cut energy costs and stop fielding tenant complaints. Our clients typically see 15-25% energy savings."

---

### 3.2 The 5-Minute Demo Pitch

Structure for a live demo or video call:

```
Minute 1 — THE PROBLEM (make them nod)
  "Right now, how do you monitor your [freezers/machines/building]?
   Let me guess: someone walks around with a clipboard every few hours?
   What happens at 2 AM on a Saturday?"

Minute 2 — THE SOLUTION (show, don't tell)
  Open your dashboard on a phone.
  "This is one of our client's dashboards. 12 freezers, all monitored 24/7.
   See this one? It's at -18C, which is normal. This one is at -14C — the
   system caught it drifting up 2 hours ago and sent an email alert.
   They fixed it before any product was affected."

Minute 3 — HOW IT WORKS (make it feel easy)
  "We install a small sensor in each unit. It connects to WiFi and sends
   data every 30 seconds. You log in here, see everything. Set your own
   alert thresholds. Get emails when something's wrong."

Minute 4 — THE PROOF (credibility)
  "We're currently monitoring [N] devices for [N] companies.
   [Client name] saved [specific number] after catching a compressor
   failure before it destroyed $50K of inventory."
   (If no clients yet: "We've been testing for [N] weeks with zero
   data loss and 99.9% uptime.")

Minute 5 — THE ASK (next step)
  "I'd like to install 2-3 sensors in your facility for a free 2-week pilot.
   You'll see your data on day one. If it works for you, we'll talk pricing.
   If not, I'll take the sensors back, no charge. When can I come by?"
```

## 4. What Features Clients Value Most

### 4.1 Feature Value Ranking (from actual IoT customer feedback patterns)

| Rank | Feature | Why They Care | Willingness to Pay |
|:---:|---------|---------------|:---:|
| 1 | **Alerts that actually work** | "Wake me up at 3 AM if something goes wrong" | High |
| 2 | **Works on my phone** | Check from bed, from vacation, from the car | High |
| 3 | **Easy to understand dashboard** | "I'm not technical. Show me green = good, red = bad." | High |
| 4 | **Compliance reports (PDF/CSV)** | "I need this for my audit next month" | High |
| 5 | **Reliability / uptime** | "If the monitoring system goes down, what's the point?" | High |
| 6 | **Fast setup** | "I don't want a 6-month IT project" | Medium |
| 7 | **Alert history / audit trail** | "Show me what happened last Tuesday at 4 PM" | Medium |
| 8 | **Multiple users with different access** | "My technicians see their area, I see everything" | Medium |
| 9 | **Device online/offline status** | "Is the sensor working or did it fall off the wall?" | Medium |
| 10 | **Custom alert thresholds per device** | "Freezer A is -18C, Freezer B is -22C — different limits" | Medium |
| 11 | **Snooze / maintenance windows** | "Don't alert me during planned maintenance" | Low |
| 12 | **Webhook / API integration** | "I want it in our Slack / ERP / ticketing system" | Low (but sells enterprise) |
| 13 | **Predictive maintenance** | "Tell me before it breaks" | Low (aspirational — they buy it but rarely use it) |
| 14 | **Beautiful charts** | Looks good in a presentation to management | Low |
| 15 | **Dark mode** | Nice-to-have | Negligible |

**Key insight:** Clients pay for alerts + reliability + compliance. They do NOT pay for pretty dashboards or advanced analytics. Those are differentiators, not drivers.

---

### 4.2 Features That Win Deals vs Features That Close Deals

| Features That WIN the Deal | Features That CLOSE the Deal |
|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| "You'll get an alert at 3 AM before product is lost" | Free pilot / proof-of-concept |
| "This report will pass your next audit" | Simple pricing (per device/month) |
| "Set up in 1 day, not 6 months" | No long-term contract |
| "Here's what it looks like on your phone" (live demo) | Reference from similar company |
| "We caught a failure that would have cost $50K" (case study) | Money-back guarantee or free trial |

## 5. Pricing Strategy

### 5.1 Pricing Models

| Model | How It Works | Best For |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Per device/month** | $15-50/device/month. Simple, scales with usage. | Standard offering. Most IoT platforms use this. |
| **Tiered per org** | $99/mo (up to 10 devices), $249/mo (up to 50), $499/mo (up to 200) | Clients who want predictable costs |
| **Hardware + subscription** | Sell sensor ($50-150 one-time) + monitoring ($10-25/device/month) | When you provide the hardware |
| **Value-based** | Price based on what they protect. $500/mo to monitor $2M of inventory. | Enterprise, high-value equipment |

### 5.2 Recommended Starting Pricing

```
PILOT:          Free for 2 weeks, 2-3 devices
STARTER:        $29/device/month  (1-10 devices, email support)
PROFESSIONAL:   $19/device/month  (11-50 devices, priority support, PDF reports)
ENTERPRISE:     Custom             (50+ devices, SLA, dedicated support, API access)

Hardware:       $75-150/sensor (if you provide it)
                OR "bring your own device" (BYOD)

Setup fee:      $0 for self-install
                $200-500 for on-site installation
```

**Volume discount signals:** The per-device price drops as count increases — this incentivizes clients to monitor MORE things, which increases stickiness.

### 5.3 Pricing Psychology

- **Never charge per alert.** Clients will set fewer alerts to save money. Defeats the purpose.
- **Never charge per user.** Clients will share logins. You lose audit trail value.
- **Always include unlimited alerts and users** in the base price. Charge per device.
- **Annual prepay discount (15-20%)** — improves your cash flow, reduces churn.
- **"Less than a coffee a day"** — $29/mo = $0.97/day. Less than the cost of one manual temperature check.

## 6. How to Win

### 6.1 The Pilot-to-Contract Pipeline

```
Step 1: IDENTIFY — Find companies with the pain (LinkedIn, directories, referrals)
                    Time: ongoing

Step 2: CONNECT  — Reach out with a personalized message about their specific situation
                    "I noticed you run 8 cold storage facilities in [region]..."
                    Time: 5 minutes per prospect

Step 3: DEMO     — 15-minute video call. Show YOUR dashboard with REAL data.
                    Not slides. Not mockups. Real devices, real charts.
                    Time: 15-30 minutes

Step 4: PILOT    — "Let me install 2-3 sensors for free. You'll see data in 1 hour."
                    Time: 1-2 hours on-site

Step 5: VALUE    — After 1 week, send: "Here's what we caught this week.
                    Here's your compliance report. Here's what would have happened
                    without monitoring."
                    Time: 30 minutes

Step 6: CLOSE    — "Ready to roll out to all [N] units? Here's the pricing."
                    Time: 1 meeting

Step 7: EXPAND   — After 3 months: "You're monitoring temperature. Want to add
                    humidity? Power? Door sensors? We can also monitor [other site]."
                    Time: ongoing
```

**The free pilot is your most powerful sales tool.** Once they see their own data, they can't unsee it. Removing it feels like going blind.

---

### 6.2 Competitive Advantages to Emphasize

| What You Say | Why It Wins |
|-------------|-----------|
| "You'll see data within 1 hour of installation" | Competitors take weeks/months for enterprise deployments |
| "No IT project required" | Competitors need VPN, firewall rules, on-prem servers |
| "You own your data — export anytime, no lock-in" | Competitors trap data in proprietary formats |
| "We set up your alerts — you don't need to figure out the software" | Competitors hand over a login and disappear |
| "One dashboard for all your sites" | Many competitors are per-site only |
| "Works with any sensor that speaks MQTT" | Competitors lock you into their hardware |
| "Pay monthly, cancel anytime" | Competitors require 3-year contracts |
| "I'll personally handle your support" (early stage) | Competitors route you through a call center |

### 6.3 Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|---------|
| "We already do manual checks" | "Manual checks miss nights, weekends, and holidays. Our clients find that 80% of incidents happen outside business hours. Let me show you what that looks like with data." |
| "It's too expensive" | "How much did your last equipment failure cost? Our clients typically see ROI from a single prevented incident. At $29/device/month, you're paying less than one hour of a technician's time to monitor 24/7." |
| "We tried IoT before and it didn't work" | "What went wrong? (Listen.) Most IoT projects fail because they're overengineered. Ours works out of the box — 1 hour setup, data flowing immediately. That's why we offer a free pilot." |
| "I need to talk to my boss" | "Absolutely. Would it help if I put together a 1-page ROI summary you can share? Or I can join a 15-minute call with them to answer questions." |
| "What about data security?" | "Your data is hosted on Azure, encrypted in transit and at rest, with per-organization isolation. Each client's data is completely separate. I can share our security overview document." |
| "We're too small" | "Our smallest client has 3 sensors. Our pricing starts at $29/device — that's $87/month for peace of mind on your 3 most critical assets. Would you like to try it?" |
| "Can it integrate with our existing system?" | "Yes — we have a webhook API that can push alerts to any system (Slack, Teams, your ERP, your ticketing system). And CSV/PDF export for reporting." |
| "What if the internet goes down?" | "The sensor stores data locally and syncs when the connection returns. You'll also get an alert that the connection was lost, so you know to check manually." |

## 7. What Most IoT Companies Do Wrong

### 7.1 Product Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Build for engineers, not operators** | Dashboard full of technical jargon. The ops manager doesn't know what "standard deviation" means. | Green = good, yellow = warning, red = bad. Plain English alerts. |
| **Too many features, none finished** | "We do monitoring, analytics, ML, digital twins, edge computing..." but alerts don't work reliably. | Do alerts + dashboards + reports PERFECTLY. Add features later. |
| **Require an IT project to deploy** | "You need to set up a VPN, open ports 1883 and 8086, configure your firewall..." Client gives up. | WiFi sensor → cloud. Zero IT involvement. |
| **Sell hardware they don't manufacture** | Buying $5 sensors from Alibaba, marking up to $150, dealing with returns and firmware bugs. | Partner with established sensor manufacturers OR use commodity sensors with proven firmware. |
| **Over-promise on "AI/ML"** | "Our AI predicts failures 30 days in advance!" — with 2 weeks of data from 3 sensors. | Don't mention AI until you have 6+ months of data and proven predictions. Call it "smart alerting" instead. |
| **Ignore the boring stuff** | No export, no audit trail, no PDF report. But they have a 3D digital twin. | Build the boring features. They close deals. |
| **No offline/resilience story** | System goes down → client loses trust forever | Build reliability from day one. LWT for device status, buffering for network outages. |

### 7.2 Sales Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Sell technology, not outcomes** | "We use MQTT, InfluxDB, and Grafana" — client's eyes glaze over. | "You'll get a phone alert before your freezer fails." |
| **Skip the pilot** | Ask for a $50K annual contract on the first meeting. | Free 2-week pilot. Let the data sell for you. |
| **Target the wrong buyer** | Sell to the CEO of a 5,000-person company. 12-month procurement process. | Sell to the ops manager of a 200-person company. 2-week decision. |
| **No case studies** | "Trust us, it works." | Document every win. Numbers, quotes, photos. |
| **Compete on price** | "We're cheaper than [competitor]." Race to the bottom. | Compete on ease of use, speed to value, and support quality. |
| **Disappear after the sale** | Install → invoice → gone. | First month: weekly check-in. "Any alerts you want to adjust? Any devices to add?" |
| **No referral ask** | Happy client = wasted opportunity. | "You seem happy with the system. Do you know anyone else who might benefit?" |
| **Generic outreach** | "Dear Sir/Madam, our IoT platform..." | "Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] operates [N] cold storage facilities in [City]. I work with similar companies and helped [Client] reduce temperature excursion incidents by 90%. Would you be open to a 15-minute call?" |

### 7.3 Business Model Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **One-time sale (hardware only)** | No recurring revenue. Must constantly find new clients. | Subscription model. Hardware is the hook, monitoring is the revenue. |
| **Free tier too generous** | 50 devices free. Nobody ever upgrades. | Free pilot (time-limited), then paid. Or max 2 devices free. |
| **Custom development per client** | Client A wants feature X, Client B wants feature Y. You're a consulting company now. | Build a product, not custom projects. Say no to one-off requests. Put them on the roadmap. |
| **No churn monitoring** | Client cancels → surprised. | Track: login frequency, alert response time, devices active. If they stop logging in → call them. |
| **Underpricing** | $5/device/month. Can't afford support, infra, or development. | $19-49/device/month. The value is in prevented losses, not in the software. |
